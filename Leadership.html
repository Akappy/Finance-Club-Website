Who

Alec Kaplan, Founder and Co-President
Alec is an ardent investor who has been trading on the stock market since he was nine years old. His fascination with investing led him to establish the Finance Club in order to fill the gap in the Lab School left by the lack of access to economics, business, and finance. Alec hopes to introduce students of all ages to the incredible world of Wall Street. With his exceptional organizational and leadership abilities, Alec plans to lay a solid foundation for the Finance Club and create a sustainable framework to ensure the long-term success of the organization.
Schuler Small, Founder and Co-President
Schuler is a passionate leader who thrives on bringing together hardworking individuals with a shared interest. He became interested in the stock market a few years ago through his father’s investing, and has never looked back. Schuler believes that understanding the stock market and its effects are paramount to succeeding in any capacity of the business world. Schuler possesses important experience from his individual investing, which he brings to the table along with his fiery personality and creativity. In the Finance Club, Schuler hopes to examine and tackle the real-world problems that fascinate him.
Jonathan Lipman, Chief Information Officer
Jonathan is an avid problem solver who sees finance as a fun and exciting problem solving game played on a worldwide scale. He had extensive experience in computer science which he applies to finance both analytically and in the sense that the analytical and logical thinking behind computer science can enhance and guide his thinking around finance. Jonathan also believes that this same thinking can be applied to leadership dynamics.
Caleb Hill, Chief Risk Officer
Caleb is a self-starting, calculating student. Always looking for the best solution, Caleb goes home long after everyone else. He was introduced into finance and the stock market by his uncle who inspired him to follow in his foot steps and pursue a career in the field. Caleb brings a valuable short-term viewpoint to the Finance Club through his investing experience.
Micaiah Buchheim-Jurisson, Chief Strategy Officer
Micaiah tries to understand how seemingly unrelated world events impact both individual stocks and the market as a whole. He first became interested in investing as an eleven-year-old during the Great Recession of 2007-09, when he invested in AIG following the government’s bailout and doubled his money within a year. Since then, he has been trading regularly. Along with ingenuity and experience, Micaiah brings knowledge as a leader and strategist in a variety of other areas to the Finance Club Board.
